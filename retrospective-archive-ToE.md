# Retrospective — archived copy (ToE case study)

Removed from `case-study-toe.html` when the final section became "Earlier explorations and releases". Kept here in case the retrospective is wanted again later.

**Section label:** Retrospective

**Headline:** It changed how I hand off to engineers, and left the business a dataset it has not used yet

---

## Impact on me

*Present-mode summary:* A finished file holds everything the builder needs, not just correct visuals. Rank metrics before a test, and every decision carried a systems dimension.

Three learnings stand out. About halfway through, engineers kept coming back with questions about interactions the Figma files did not answer, which changed how I think about a finished file: it is done when it holds everything the person building it needs, not when the designs look right. The failed experiment taught me to rank metrics before running a test, not after. And designing against a design system that did not exist yet meant every decision carried both a product and a systems dimension, slower, but grounded in real implementation.

---

## Impact on process

*Present-mode summary:* The handoff lesson became a team session on Dev Mode and a shared Ready for development signal.

The handoff learning did not stay my own habit. It became a session with the whole front-end engineering team on Figma's Dev Mode and on marking designs as "Ready for development" as a shared signal. What began as me fixing my own handoffs became a convention the team works to.

---

## Impact on users

*Present-mode summary:* Two mandatory steps added, completion held near 90%. Availability rose about 4 points and Profile Boost conversion roughly doubled.

This is where the real numbers sit. The redesign added two mandatory steps, and completion still held at about 90% before and after. Availability rose about 4 percentage points, from about 70% to about 74%. Profile Boost conversion roughly doubled, from about 17% to about 33%, with richer data, about 6.5 vendors and 3.4 categories per expert, up from 4.2 and 2.0. One number I would have wanted and do not have is how many experts actually reviewed their current experience during signing, which was never tracked.

---

## Impact on the business

*Present-mode summary:* Lower compliance and eligibility risk now, plus a structured dataset of expert knowledge the business has not yet used to improve matching.

The realized win was lowering compliance and eligibility risk: the Tutorial and Questionnaire now sit in one reviewed flow, and current experience is prominent, so experts are properly screened before they reach a client. The project also laid a foundation the business has not cashed in yet. Profile Boost has captured a structured dataset of what experts actually know since launch, and v2 made it far more complete, but using it to match experts to better-fitting projects is a separate initiative still ahead. Naming both the asset and the gap is the honest read.
